01 What is mikrokinesitherapy?

+

The human body is a remarkable system that, thanks to its complexity and adaptability, is able to counteract a wide spectrum of negative external influences such as physical, emotional, toxic, viral, bacterial, parasitic and environmental traumas. The key feature enabling the body to survive and regenerate is its internal defense mechanism and capacity for self-repair.

However, when the intensity of external aggression exceeds these natural defensive capabilities, the vitality of the affected tissues becomes disrupted. This is the moment when the body "remembers" the trauma, leading to changes in tissue function that can manifest locally or in other, distant areas of the body.

Mikrokinesitherapy is the answer to these challenges. Through a specialized manual micropalpation technique, therapists are able to locate and identify the traces left by various aggressions in the body's tissues. The central element of this method is the manual stimulation of the body's auto-corrective mechanisms, aimed at preventing further tissue degradation and restoring their proper vitality and function.

Thanks to its universality, mikrokinesitherapy is effective for patients of all ages, whether they suffer from existing conditions or the therapy serves a preventive purpose. This method enables not only the treatment of manifest health problems but also their prevention through early detection and elimination of tissue function blockages.

02 Genesis of the method

+

Mikrokinesitherapy is a therapeutic method characterized by subtle intervention in the body's structure. It enables the elimination of traces of traumas acquired by the individual during their lifetime from the body's structures.

The human body exhibits remarkable flexibility and capacity for self-regeneration, allowing it to adapt to diverse situations and independently cope with damage resulting from various forms of aggression such as pollution, emotional stress, physical injuries, etc. When the intensity of trauma exceeds the body's defensive capabilities, these traumas become recorded in the cells, creating a kind of "scar" in cellular memory. The accumulation of such scars can lead to the development of diseases or pain.

The theoretical foundation of mikrokinesitherapy is the relationship between internal organs and specific points on the skin, which stems from embryonic development. Stimulation of specific points on the body triggers reactions in internal organs, muscles and tissues, which in turn contributes to increased immunity, supporting the body in combating the negative effects of medications, chemotherapy, and accelerating recovery after injuries or surgeries.

Thanks to its gentleness, this method is also suitable for children and infants, helping to alleviate or eliminate symptoms such as bloating, restlessness, allergies, respiratory diseases and sleep problems.

03 How does mikrokinesitherapy work?

+

Mikrokinesitherapy, utilizing subtle mobilization techniques, engages the patient's nervous system in the healing process, triggering the body's natural regenerative mechanisms. The therapist, through precisely applied pressure to selected skin areas, initiates a chain reaction that begins with transmitting a stimulus to the nervous system and then to the cerebral cortex.

The brain, in response to the received signal, generates impulses aimed at releasing muscle tension and correcting internal organ dysfunction, which contributes to reducing pain syndromes and improving overall health.

The foundation of mikrokinesitherapy's action is the concept of cellular memory, which refers to the body's ability to "remember" traumas regardless of their nature -- physical, emotional or toxic. The human body, being highly adaptive, attempts to independently counteract the effects of traumas by mobilizing internal forces to fight negative consequences. However, when the influence of negative stimuli exceeds defensive capabilities, disease may develop.

Importantly, the effectiveness of mikrokinesitherapy is not dependent on the time elapsed since the trauma occurred, nor on the patient's conscious memory of the event. This opens broad possibilities for treating various health problems, even those that are long-standing or hidden.

04 The procedure -- stages

+

Stage 1 -- Diagnostic micropalpation: The therapist, using gentle touch, identifies areas of tension and disharmony in the patient's body, locating the source of disturbances known as the pathogenic scar. This is crucial for diagnosis and the further therapeutic plan.

Stage 2 -- Tissue identification: The therapist searches for symptoms related to the identified scar while maintaining contact with the site of its occurrence. Moving the other hand along the body's midline, they seek to find and define the damaged tissue at the appropriate "level" of the body. This method also allows for an approximate determination of when the trauma occurred.

Stage 3 -- Auto-correction stimulation: Stimulation of the identified pathogenic scar initiates immediate auto-corrective mechanisms, activating tissue memory and supporting natural self-healing processes. This direct interaction with body tissues is effective in adults, children and animals alike, indicating the universality and effectiveness of this method.

05 What happens after therapy?

+

After therapy, temporary symptoms may occur such as fatigue or intensified complaints, which is the body's natural response to recalling and eliminating long-standing disturbances. The typical body response to the elimination of trauma traces is relaxation and drowsiness.

Rest and drinking plenty of water are recommended to support the body's elimination processes.

The number of necessary sessions is individually adjusted, depending on the patient's response to treatment. Mikrokinesitherapy also offers preventive therapy and support in cases of evolving diseases, which underscores its versatility and potential in promoting health and the body's self-regulation.

+

The method supports the treatment of many diseases by eliminating their causes. It is helpful in combating or minimizing various types of pain such as back pain, joint pain, and headaches. It reduces nervous tension, alleviates symptoms of depression, and supports vitality.
